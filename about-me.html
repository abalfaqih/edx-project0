The City/Town of Shibam
A UNESCO World Heritage Site
If you want to see a photo gallery of Shibam, UNISCO has a great photo gallery of it here

My name is Ahmed. I was born many many years ago in this interesting city called Shibam, in South Yemen then, or Yemen as it is known now. It is interesting for many reasons. Some people call it the oldest sky scraper twon in the world. Others call it the Manahaten of the desert. It is also called the Yellow City, because it is always sunny. But the most interesting part of it is, most houses are linked to each other via a secret door. This way, women do not have to leave the house to visit their neighbours. They just go through the secret door and avoid having to climb down and up 5 floors or more on foot (ther are no lifts in these buildings). The other interesing part is that all of the houses are made of mud. These building stood the tests of times for over 300 years. Rain or shine. They stood tall and strong, but non are taller than Trump Tower
Currently, and have been for the last 25 years, I have been living in two cities mainly, Singapore and Malaysia. I shuttle beteen these two citis approximately twice a month by bus mainly. Each trip takes about 6 houts.
For the record, I left the city of Shibam town in 1973, and have never gone back.
